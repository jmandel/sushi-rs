4. Observation.subject
Definition

Optional reference to the Patient resource for the person whose period-tracking data is represented. The Bundle is still scoped to one person when Patient resources and subject references are omitted.

ShortOptional person reference
Comments

One would expect this element to be a cardinality of 1..1. The only circumstance in which the subject can be missing is when the observation is made by a device that does not know the patient. In this case, the observation SHALL be matched to a patient through some context/channel matching technique, and at this point, the observation should be updated.

6. Observation.effective[x]
Definition

The date or time associated with the source fact. Producers MAY use date precision (for example, 2026-06-24) when the source stores a calendar-day fact, or full timestamp precision when the source stores a specific time. Producers SHALL NOT invent a time solely to satisfy this profile.

ShortDate or timestamp for the source fact
Comments

At least a date should be present unless this observation is a historical report. For recording imprecise or "fuzzy" times (For example, a blood glucose measurement taken "after breakfast") use the Timing datatype which allow the measurement to be tied to regular life events.
